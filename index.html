Demographics & Statistics

Out of the estimated 176,000 people in the area, about 62% are Hispanic and 30% are Black.
As of 2022, about 36% of the population lives below the poverty line and the median household income is $31,990.
Although Belmont is home to many college students from Fordham University, the rate of college-educated indivudals is lower than the rest of the city.
The area also has higher rates of obesity, diabetes, and high blood pressure compared to city averages. Regarding housing, most people rent multi-unit homes.
The median year in which properties were built is 1953 and the average rent for a 2-bedroom apartment is $2,300.
